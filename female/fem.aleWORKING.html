FEM.ALE - Brewster Festival 23-25 May
// A weekend of beer and music celebrating women in the brewing industry // Friday 23rd - Sunday 25th May //

// Why Female Ale? //

Women enjoying beer is no new thing in Norfolk – the myths that women don't enjoy beer or that pubs are a space for men to escape find no place in one of the finest cities in the country to find a pint. But while we know beer is loved by men and women drinkers alike, at FEM.ALE we also want to celebrate women who are doing the brewing within the beer industry.

So, as part of the City of Ale Festival, which each year provides a showcase for the pubs and breweries of Norwich and their selection of craft beers, the Plasterers Arms is holding our first FEM.ALE event, a weekend dedicated to beer brewed by women that's enjoyed by everyone. dedicated to female brewed ales, featuring one from an Adnams brewster crafted specially for the event.

Friday night will be kicking off the weekend with live music from local female musicians and DJs provided by Pony Up and tweeOFF! promoter Rosie Arnold. On Saturday we'll be looking into the industry itself, with a panel of four invited brewsters discussing their experiences as women in the industry and what they love about brewing beer. We'll also be holding a tasting of their beers so we can find out where their skills go! We're planning more ways to celebrate these great beers and the women who make them over the weekend which we'll be shouting about on the lead up to the City of Ale Festival 2014. We want everyone to feel welcome at FEM.ALE so stand by for more info and updates and we'll see you in May!
