BRITAIN'S FIRST BREWSTER BEER FESTIVAL RETURNS TO NORFOLK 22-25 MAY 2015

22ND-25TH MAY 2015 at THE PLASTERERS ARMS, NORWICH

After the runaway success of last year's Festival, FEM.ALE returns for its second annual flagship festival - and this time it's going to be even bigger and better!

Women enjoying beer is no new thing - the myths that women don't enjoy beer or that pubs are just a space for men to escape to, find no place in one of the finest cities in the country to find a pint. But while we know beer is loved by men and women alike, FEM.ALE celebrates women who are actually doing the brewing within the industry. As part of the City of Ale Festival, the Plasterers Arms is once again devoting a weekend to beer brewed by women, and enjoyed by everyone.

For the May Day bank holiday weekend, all 15 of the Plasterers' gravity pumps will be dedicated to female brewed ales, featuring two unique beers specially crafted for us by beer technologist Sophie de Ronde and Adnams brewster Belinda Jennings. This year we will again be teaming up with musical hell-raisers Pony Up who will be providing live music from local female musicians and DJs.

We'll also be welcoming back award winning beer writer, sommelier and booze historian Jane Peyton for a female beer and female cheese matching evenings Cheeses. There'll be the opportunity to meet Brewsters Lizzie Ellis from Lincolnshire's Horncastle Ales and from London brewery Hop Stuff, Alix Shaw.

Everyone is welcome at FEM.ALE so we invite you all to come, drink and be merry!

Muntons' Honey Spiced Ale


Spiced Honey Pale is a truly unique beer in every way - it's the first publicly available beer from Muntons, the Stowmarket-based malt supplier. It was created by Sophie De Ronde, mastermind of International Women's Collaboration Day and ex-head brewer at Brentwood Brewery.

The beer has been made using honey, ginger and coriander seeds, Vienna malt as a backdrop with a hint of caramalt, toasted wheat and rye for a rich golden, toasted maltiness.

Sounds delicious, doesn't it? We can't wait!

Muntons are kindly donating their proceeds from this beer to charity.

Look at how many people joined in and brewed Unite Red Ale on International Women's Collaboration Brew Day!


We've got a cask of the one that Jaega from Wild Card Brewery in East London made with Sophie De Ronde (from Muntons Malt) and a bunch of other excellent ladies. We've also now just confirmed that we have the Unite Red Saison from Gyle 59 too! A great opportunity to try different takes on the base recipe from two very different breweries.

International Women's Collaboration Brew Day is the brain child of Sophie De Ronde (who is also making us a beer especially for our event). The concept is that any brewsters who want to join in simply brew their own version of a recipe, on the same day as other participating breweries. Each brewster adds their own little spin or extra ingredient, for an original take on a blueprint shared across the world.

Loads of breweries from eleven different countries were involved, with brewsters in Australia, Brazil, Canada, Japan, Italy, Mexico, New Zealand, Sweden and Switzerland and USA brewing on the same day, and to the same recipe as our folks over here in Great Britain.
